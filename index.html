Zakat Calculator

Zakat is the 3rd Pillar of Islam and a way to attain internal supplication. In Islamic Shariya, Zakat is obligatory when a certain amount of money, called the Nisab (نِصاب), is reached or exceeded. The Nisab was set by Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) at a rate equivalent to: 87.48 grams of gold and 612.36 grams of silver. Zakat is calculated as 2.5% of the total wealth after subtracting your liabilities.

FAQ

How can I calculate my Zakat?

Firstly, find out your net worth, which is the total wealth that you've as cash, given as loans, deposited wealth, gold, silver, or anything as stock. Then subtract your liabilities from your net worth and find out the Nisab amount for your country for the particular lunar year. If your total wealth exceeds or reaches nisab, then multiply your total wealth by 0.025, that's your payable zakat amount. If your wealth is lower than nisab, then you don't need to pay zakat. However, the nisab amount varies every year for every country, so it's always a good practice to double-check the nisab value.

Is Zakat always 2.5 percent?

Yes, adult Muslims who are eligible to pay zakat need to find out 2.5% of their wealth, the result is equivalent to the zakat they need to pay once a year. However, to be eligible to pay zakat, you must ensure that your net worth reaches the minimum threshold, which is essentially the nisab.
